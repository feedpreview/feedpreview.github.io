About This Tool

The Most Accurate Twitter / X Post Preview Tool

Whether you call it Twitter or X, our free preview tool gives you a real-time, pixel-accurate simulation of how your tweet will look in the actual feed — before you ever hit Post. No more surprises when you publish.

Our tool faithfully replicates Twitter's Light, Dark, and Dim themes, including typography, action bar layout, verified badges, engagement rows, and all native photo grid configurations. It also supports advanced features like polls with live vote percentages, threads with connecting lines, quote tweets, and reply previews.

Everything processes locally in your browser. Your text and photos are never sent to any server. Complete privacy, always.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Twitter / X post preview tool.

Is this Twitter preview tool free? +
Yes, completely free. No account, no subscription, no watermarks on screenshots. Use it unlimited times.
How many photos can I add to the tweet preview? +
Up to 4 photos, which matches Twitter/X's real photo limit. They are automatically arranged in Twitter's authentic 1, 2, 3, and 4-image grid layouts.
Can I preview Twitter polls? +
Yes! Select "Poll" in the Post Type tab, add up to 4 options with custom vote counts, and watch the percentage bars render in real-time in the preview.
Can I preview a Twitter thread? +
Yes. Go to the Type tab, select Thread, and add multiple tweet texts. They will render with Twitter's connecting thread lines between each tweet.
Does the preview support Twitter Dark Mode? +
Yes. The Style tab offers all three of Twitter's official themes: Light, Dark (pure black), and Dim (dark blue-grey). Switch between them instantly.
Is my data stored anywhere? +
Never. All previewing runs entirely inside your browser. Your photos, text, and profile details are never uploaded to any server.
Can I preview RTL languages like Arabic? +
Yes. Switch Language Direction to "Right to Left (RTL)" in the Style tab to preview how your tweet reads in RTL languages like Arabic or Hebrew.
